## 1. System Classification

**System Type:** Hybrid — Structured Data Extraction (`ba-srs-writer` → canonical `requirements.json`) + Multi-Agent verification loop (independent fresh-context `ba-critic` Chain-of-Verification).

**Description:**
Two prompt-defined LLM operators, run by the host agent runtime (Codex/GPT in v1; Claude in v2), turn an arbitrary source doc into atomic, grounded, verifiable requirements. `ba-srs-writer` reads pre-split source sections and emits canonical `requirements.json` (assigning `FR-`/`NFR-`/`BR-` IDs, each `stated` requirement carrying a verbatim `source_trace` `{doc, span, section}`), from which `ba-tools` deterministically renders the IEEE-830 SRS `.md`. `ba-critic` then runs a fresh-context CoVe pass: it re-derives each requirement's truth from the **source doc only** (never reading the writer's `analysis.md`/rationale, never editing), driving a ≤3-revision loop that early-exits on convergence. Users are Business Analysts working in the Codex chat; the deterministic `ba-tools verify` is the hard gate (exit 2), and the critic's unresolved FAIL findings block convergence. "Good" = every `stated` requirement is atomic, unambiguous, and its `source_trace.span` is a real ≥12-char verbatim substring of the cited source section — so the REQ-ID → source_trace → INDEX traceability spine holds.

**Critical Failure Modes:**
1. **Ungrounded requirement passes the gate** — `ba-srs-writer` emits a `stated` requirement whose `source_trace.span` is fabricated or paraphrased (not a verbatim substring of the cited source section), and it slips through because the critic failed to catch it. Breaks the core traceability guarantee.
2. **Critic loses independence** — `ba-critic` reads the writer's rationale/`analysis.md` (or is fed the writer's reasoning) instead of re-deriving from the source, collapsing CoVe into self-agreement and rubber-stamping the draft.
3. **False convergence / silent auto-pass** — the loop reports "converged" while unresolved FAIL-severity findings remain, or exceeds 3 loops and silently passes instead of escalating to a human Confirm checkpoint.
4. **Non-atomic or mis-classified requirements** — compound "and/or" requirements, vague/unverifiable statements, or wrong `FR-`/`NFR-`/`BR-` prefix assignment that corrupts the REQ-ID registry downstream operators depend on.
5. **Determinism-boundary breach** — any LLM/judgement call leaking into `ba-tools` itself (verify, trace write, index update must stay 100% hash-provable and reproducible).

## 1b. Domain Context

> Researched by `gsd-domain-researcher`. Grounds the evaluation strategy in domain expert knowledge.

**Industry Vertical:** Business analysis / requirements engineering — specifically formal SRS/BRD authoring and requirements traceability. This is a domain-agnostic BA practice tool: the *source documents* it processes may come from any vertical (the BA's client domain), but the *craft being evaluated* is requirements engineering itself.

**User Population:** Business Analysts and requirements engineers working interactively in the Codex chat. They feed in a source document (meeting notes, project brief, use case, draft spec) and consume the emitted `requirements.json` + IEEE-830 SRS/BRD. Downstream consumers of the output are QA/test leads (who write test cases against the requirements), developers (who implement), and project stakeholders who sign off on scope.

**Stakes Level:** High. Not Critical, because this is a general-purpose internal BA operator with a mandatory human-in-the-loop (the writer drafts, the BA reviews, escalation-to-Confirm on non-convergence) and it is **not certified for any regulated regime** (see Regulatory section). It is rated High rather than Medium because requirements are the upstream root artifact of the SDLC: a defective requirement propagates into design, code, and test, and the cost to correct grows by roughly an order of magnitude at each downstream stage (a long-standing requirements-engineering observation). A fabricated or ambiguous requirement that survives the gate corrupts every downstream artifact that cites its REQ-ID.

**Output Consequence:** The `requirements.json` is the canonical source of truth (D-01); the SRS/BRD and the REQ-ID registry are rendered from it, and downstream operators (Mermaid diagrams, UI mockups, backlog stories in later phases) trace against its REQ-IDs. When a BA acts on this output they: (a) treat each `stated` requirement as faithfully sourced from the input doc, (b) hand REQ-IDs to QA for test design, and (c) rely on the INDEX.md matrix to surface scope gaps, orphan citations, and stale (source-drifted) requirements. If an ungrounded or non-atomic requirement passes, it enters this chain as if it were trustworthy.

### What Domain Experts Evaluate Against

> Practitioner quality characteristics for "good requirements," drawn from IEEE-830-1998 (SRS-level characteristics), ISO/IEC/IEEE 29148:2018 (its successor; per-requirement characteristics), and BABOK requirement-quality criteria. Each is grounded in this project's own machine-checkable definitions where they exist (D-03 grounding rule, D-09 classification prefixes, Phase 1 lint heuristics).

| Dimension | Good (expert accepts) | Bad (expert flags) | Stakes | Source |
|-----------|----------------------|--------------------|--------|--------|
| **Groundedness / traceability to source** | Every `stated` requirement cites a real ≥12-char verbatim substring of the named source section; a reviewer can find that exact text in the source. `derived` requirements are honestly labeled and carry no false citation. | A requirement asserted as `stated` whose `source_trace.span` is paraphrased, summarized, or invented (not present verbatim in the cited section); or an inference dressed up as a sourced fact. | Critical | ISO 29148 *necessary*/*traceable*; IEEE-830 *traceable*; project D-03 + `citation_exists` (≥12-char verbatim, section-scoped, exit 2 `CITATION_NOT_FOUND`). |
| **Atomicity / singularity** | One testable requirement per entry. Compound intent is split into separate REQ-IDs. Uses a single normative "shall" per statement. | A single entry bundling multiple obligations via "and"/"or"/"as well as"; a statement that two testers would split into two test cases. | High | ISO 29148 *singular*; BABOK atomic requirements; project Phase 1 lint atomicity check. |
| **Unambiguity (no weak/vague language)** | Precise, single-interpretation wording; quantified where a quality is claimed ("responds within 2 s" not "fast"); avoids weak words ("user-friendly", "efficient", "appropriate", "etc.", "as needed", "support"). | Vague quantifiers, subjective adjectives, open-ended "etc.", or terms two readers would interpret differently. | High | IEEE-830 *unambiguous* (one interpretation per requirement); ISO 29148 *unambiguous*; project Phase 1 lint ambiguity check. |
| **Verifiability / testability** | A reviewer can name a concrete test, inspection, or measurement that would confirm the requirement is met; success conditions are objective. | Untestable quality attributes ("system shall be reliable / maintainable / intuitive") with no measurable acceptance criterion; aspirational statements with no pass/fail boundary. | High | IEEE-830 *verifiable*; ISO 29148 *verifiable*; project Phase 1 lint verifiability check. |
| **Correct classification (FR / NFR / BR) + no implementation bias** | Each requirement carries the right semantic prefix — `FR-` functional behavior, `NFR-` quality attribute/constraint, `BR-` business rule/policy — and states *what* is needed, not *how* to build it (no design/technology prescription unless the source mandates it). | Wrong prefix (e.g. a performance constraint filed as `FR-`); a business policy mislabeled as a functional requirement; a requirement that dictates a specific implementation/technology the source never required (design-vs-requirement confusion). | High | ISO 29148 *appropriate*; BABOK requirement classification schema; project D-09 (`FR-`/`NFR-`/`BR-` prefixes; agent assigns, lint enforces uniqueness/stability). |
| **Completeness & consistency (set-level)** | The requirement set covers the obligations the source actually states, including non-functional ones; no internal contradictions; no unresolved "TBD". REQ-IDs are unique and stable across revisions. | Missing NFRs the source implied; contradictory requirements; "TBD" left in; the same REQ-ID silently changing statement wording between runs (drift). | Medium | IEEE-830 *complete*/*consistent*; ISO 29148 *complete*/*consistent*; project D-12 (`statement_hash` drift detection) + Phase 1 REQ-ID stability lint (TOOL-05). |

### Known Failure Modes in This Domain

> How requirements-engineering quality failures actually manifest — not generic "hallucination," but the specific shapes a senior BA scans for. Several map directly to this system's Critical Failure Modes (Section 1).

1. **Gold-plating / invented requirements.** The writer emits requirements the source never states — added "for completeness," to sound thorough, or because they are common in the writer's prior experience. In this system this surfaces as a `stated` requirement with a fabricated or paraphrased `source_trace.span` (caught by `citation_exists`) — or, more insidiously, a *real but irrelevant* verbatim span that passes the substring check yet does not actually justify the requirement (the `ba-critic` CoVe re-derivation is the intended catch for this; the deterministic gate proves the quote is *real*, not that it *justifies* the statement).
2. **Compound / non-atomic requirements.** "The system shall log in the user **and** email a receipt" — one entry that should be two REQ-IDs. Breaks test design (which test fails when only the email part breaks?) and corrupts 1:1 REQ-ID → behavior coupling.
3. **Ambiguous quantifiers / untestable quality attributes.** "fast", "secure", "scalable", "user-friendly", "as needed" — no measurable acceptance criterion, so QA cannot write a deterministic test. The most common cause of disputes at sign-off and at UAT.
4. **Requirement vs. design confusion.** Stating *how* ("use a Redis cache", "store in PostgreSQL") instead of *what* ("retrieve the profile within 200 ms"). Over-constrains the solution and smuggles unsourced architectural decisions into the requirement baseline.
5. **Verbatim restatement without atomizing.** Copying a whole source paragraph into one "requirement" — technically grounded (the span is real) but not decomposed into discrete, testable units. Passes a naive citation check while failing the singularity characteristic.
6. **Mis-citation / wrong-span grounding.** The span is real and verbatim but is pulled from the wrong section, or supports a different claim than the requirement makes. Section-scoping (`--cite-scope section`) reduces this; cross-section drift is the residual risk the critic must catch.
7. **Scope creep & missing NFRs.** Functional requirements expand beyond the source's intent, while non-functional requirements the source implied (performance, security, availability, compliance) are silently omitted — a classic asymmetry, since FRs are easier to extract than NFRs.
8. **Mis-classification (FR/NFR/BR).** A business rule filed as a functional requirement, or a constraint as functionality — corrupting the REQ-ID registry that downstream operators depend on (Section 1, failure mode #4).

## 2. Framework Decision

**Selected Framework:** Native host-runtime agent prompts (Codex-native skills, v1) + thin in-repo workflow orchestration + structured-output / Chain-of-Verification prompting pattern. **No pip-installable LLM framework.**

Authoring surface: flat `.agents/skills/ba-srs-analyze/` (SKILL.md `name`+`description` only; `agents/openai.yaml` `interface.*` + `policy.allow_implicit_invocation: false`) + a thin workflow under `ba-core/workflows/` + the `ba-core/agents/ba-srs-writer.md` & `ba-critic.md` prompt bodies. `ba-tools` (Python 3.11+, stdlib + existing `filelock`) remains the deterministic hard gate (`verify`, `trace write`, `index update`) and does **zero** LLM work.

**Version:** No framework version to pin — pattern is library-free. Runtime deps unchanged: Python 3.11+, `filelock>=3.29.4` (existing), `pytest>=9.0` (test extra). Phase 2 adds **zero new runtime dependencies**.

**Rationale:**
The host agent runtime (Codex/GPT now, Claude in v2) *is* the executor — there is no separate Python orchestration process for a framework to live in, so every pip-installable agent framework (LangChain, LangGraph, LlamaIndex, CrewAI, AutoGen, DSPy, OpenAI/Claude Agent SDKs) is structurally inapplicable, not merely deprioritized. Adding one would violate **both** the determinism boundary (`ba-tools` must be hash-provable-only, zero LLM calls — DESIGN §5/§11) **and** Phase 2's zero-new-runtime-deps rule (CONTEXT D-18). The writer/critic CoVe loop is fully expressible as prompt design + thin workflow dispatch: ≤3 revisions, read-only critic re-deriving from source, early-exit on zero new FAIL findings, escalate-to-Confirm — backed by `ba-tools verify` (exit 2) as the deterministic block. The lightest approach that respects both non-negotiables wins; this is it, and it matches DESIGN §3/§4 verbatim.

**Alternatives Considered:**

| Framework | Ruled Out Because |
|-----------|------------------|
| LangChain / LangGraph / LlamaIndex / CrewAI / AutoGen / DSPy / OpenAI Agents SDK / Claude Agent SDK | All pip-installable; would run an LLM inside a Python process. Breaks the determinism boundary (`ba-tools` must do only hash-provable work) AND the zero-new-deps rule. Also assume a Python orchestration process that does not exist — the host runtime is the executor. |
| Minimal in-repo orchestration convention (same native prompts, but the ≤3-loop / convergence / escalation made explicit as named workflow states + a `ba-tools`-tracked loop counter in `STATE.md`) | **Alternative / fallback, not eliminated.** Adds explicit auditable loop state with zero new dependency and no determinism-boundary crossing. Adopt only if loop control embedded in the thin workflow `.md` proves too implicit to audit. |

**Vendor Lock-In Accepted:** No. Agent prompt bodies and the thin workflow are runtime-agnostic; the v1→v2 transform (Codex → Claude, DESIGN §9) requires no rewrite of agent logic. `ba-tools` is pure stdlib Python with one cross-platform dep (`filelock`).

### Core Imports

There are no Python LLM imports. The only real code surface is the deterministic `ba-tools` CLI the workflow shells out to. The "imports" are the **CLI command shapes** the workflow invokes (every success → UTF-8 JSON on stdout; every error → exit `2`):

```bash
# Gate the canonical JSON directly (D-02): citation-exists + lint fold + hash-match.
ba-tools verify --reqs .ba-ops/srs/<slug>/requirements.json --reqs-format json \
                --source <path/to/source.md> [--cite-scope section|document]

# Record the artifact -> REQ-ID trace (+ per-statement hash + source hash) (TOOL-07, D-05).
ba-tools trace write --kind srs --slug <slug> \
                     --artifact .ba-ops/srs/<slug>/SRS.md \
                     --source <path/to/source.md> \
                     --req-ids .ba-ops/srs/<slug>/requirements.json

# Rebuild the traceability matrix with gap/orphan/stale detection (TOOL-08, TRACE-04/05).
ba-tools index update

# Loop state, lockfile-guarded (filelock, FileLock(timeout=10)) — survives /clear.
ba-tools state update|patch|advance ...
```

### Entry Point Pattern

The entry point is the **thin workflow dispatch** for `--route full`, NOT an SDK call. It passes **file paths + a small manifest, never raw content** (DESIGN §4, D-21). Concrete pseudo-flow / workflow skeleton:

```text
# .agents/ba-daily-operators/ba-core/workflows/ba-srs-analyze.md  (route: full)

1. RESOLVE ROUTE
   route = --route or `ba-tools resolve-route ba-srs-analyze`   # default: full
   slug  = `ba-tools` derives (--slug | slugify(source) | uc-id)   # D-19, deterministic

2. EXTRACT (deterministic)
   `ba-tools` splits source into .ba-ops/srs/<slug>/ sections via markdown_sections (D-17).

3. DRAFT  ->  spawn ba-srs-writer (FRESH CONTEXT)
   payload = { source_path, sections_dir, slug, route }      # paths only, D-21
   writer READS sections, EMITS .ba-ops/srs/<slug>/requirements.json:
     each `stated` req carries source_trace { doc, span(>=12-char verbatim), section }.
   `ba-tools` renders SRS.md + REQUIREMENTS.md deterministically FROM the JSON (D-01).

4. VERIFY (deterministic hard gate — exit 2 blocks; D-02/D-10)
   `ba-tools verify --reqs requirements.json --reqs-format json --source <source>`
   exit 2 on any FAIL  ->  loop back to DRAFT with the failures folded in.

5. CRITIC CoVe LOOP  (<=3, early-exit on convergence; D-10/D-11)
   loop n in 1..3:
     spawn ba-critic (FRESH CONTEXT)
       payload = { source_path, requirements.json path }      # ONLY these — D-21
       critic RE-DERIVES each req's truth from the SOURCE ONLY,
       NEVER reads analysis.md / writer rationale (CoVe independence).
       returns findings [{ req_id, severity: fail|warn, question, answer, verdict }].
     converged = zero NEW fail-severity findings   # D-11
     if converged: log ("passed early" if n==1 else f"passed after {n}"); BREAK
     else: spawn ba-srs-writer to re-draft; re-run VERIFY; continue
   if still failing after loop 3: ESCALATE to Confirm checkpoint (human) — NEVER silent pass (D-10)

6. TRACE + INDEX (deterministic)
   `ba-tools trace write --kind srs --slug <slug> ...`        # TOOL-07
   `ba-tools index update`                                     # TOOL-08 — gap/orphan/stale
   `ba-tools state advance`                                    # record gate verdict (lockfile-guarded)
```

### Common Pitfalls

1. **CoVe independence collapse (the #1 risk).** If the critic is fed — or chooses to read — the writer's `analysis.md`/rationale, CoVe degenerates into self-agreement and rubber-stamps the draft. The CoVe paper's measured gain comes specifically from the **factored** variant: verification questions answered *independently of the original answer*. Enforce by payload shape: the critic receives `{source_path, requirements.json path}` **only** (D-21) and is explicitly told not to read working notes. This is a critical failure mode (Section 1, #2), not a style preference.
2. **False convergence / silent auto-pass.** Reporting "converged" while unresolved FAIL findings remain, or exceeding 3 loops and passing quietly. Converged is defined narrowly: **zero new FAIL-severity findings** (D-11). After loop 3 still failing → escalate to a human Confirm checkpoint, never silent-pass (D-10). Log `"passed early"` vs `"passed after N"`.
3. **Determinism-boundary breach.** Any judgement/LLM call leaking into `ba-tools` (verify, trace write, index update) destroys hash-provability and reproducibility (DESIGN §5/§11). The CLI may *parse, hash, and substring-check*; it must never *decide whether a requirement is justified*. Rendering `SRS.md`/`REQUIREMENTS.md` from the JSON is mechanical → keep it in `ba-tools`; phrasing/classification is judgement → keep it in the agent.
4. **Citation that is paraphrased, not verbatim.** A `stated` requirement whose `source_trace.span` is a paraphrase (not a real ≥12-char substring of the cited section) is exactly what `citation_exists` rejects (`CITATION_NOT_FOUND`, exit 2). Writers must copy the span verbatim from the source section, not summarize it. Section heading normalization matters: `citation.py` matches headings case-insensitively after stripping `#`/whitespace and stops at a same-or-higher-level heading — a span that lives under a *sub*-heading of the cited section is still in scope, but one under a sibling section is not.
5. **Skill auto-firing / wrong-route inference.** Leaving `allow_implicit_invocation` unset (or `true`) lets the analysis path fire without an explicit `$` mention; inferring a route from free text when `--route` is absent is forbidden (DESIGN §11). Always set `policy.allow_implicit_invocation: false` and fall back to the *default* route (`full`) via `ba-tools resolve-route`, never a guessed one.
6. **Frontmatter / nesting drift.** Adding any field beyond `name`+`description` to SKILL.md frontmatter is silently ignored by Codex; putting `allow_implicit_invocation` at the YAML top level instead of under `policy:` (and `display_name`/`short_description`/`default_prompt` under `interface:`) breaks the contract. Both are verified in `CLAUDE.md`. Keep AGENTS.md/eager refs < 32,768 B (silent truncation).

## 4. Implementation Guidance

**Model Configuration:**

The model is the **host runtime's model** — Codex/GPT in v1, Claude in v2 — so the authoring layer is effectively **model-agnostic** (DESIGN §9; agent prompt bodies ship unchanged into v2). There is no SDK parameter surface to configure in this repo; the guidance below is the *intent* the prompts encode, applied by whatever model the host runs.

- **Task profile:** this is a **grounded structured-extraction + verification** task, not creative generation. Favor low-temperature, deterministic decoding. If/when the host exposes sampling controls (e.g., a future v2 Claude SDK path), use temperature ≈ 0–0.2 for both writer and critic so the same source yields stable spans and stable verification verdicts. Determinism at the *content* layer is desirable but not relied upon for safety — the hard guarantee is the deterministic `ba-tools verify` gate downstream, which is reproducible regardless of model sampling.
- **Output discipline:** the writer must emit **valid JSON matching the requirement schema** (Section 4b.1) with verbatim `source_trace.span` values. The critic must emit **structured findings** (`{req_id, severity, question, answer, verdict}`), severity ∈ `{fail, warn}`.
- **CoVe loop control (the real "configuration" — D-10/D-11):**
  - Max **3** revision loops.
  - Critic is **read-only**; only `ba-srs-writer` re-drafts between loops.
  - **Converged = zero new FAIL-severity findings** in a loop. WARN findings are logged, non-blocking (mirrors the verify FAIL/WARN model).
  - On convergence, log `"passed early"` (loop 1) or `"passed after N"` (N>1).
  - After loop 3 still failing → **escalate to a Confirm checkpoint** (human decides). Never silent auto-pass.
- **`max_tokens`:** set an explicit upper bound on each spawn where the host allows it (see 4b.3). The writer bound scales with source size; the critic bound is smaller (findings list, not prose).

**Core Pattern:**

Two-role **Chain-of-Verification** over a deterministic gate. The writer produces the canonical artifact; `ba-tools verify` proves citations are real and folds lint (exit 2 hard-blocks); the independent critic re-derives truth from the source only, driving a bounded revision loop with early-exit and human escalation. Pseudocode for the loop the workflow drives:

```text
draft     = spawn(ba-srs-writer, { source_path, sections_dir, slug, route })  # writes requirements.json
render    = ba-tools (deterministic) -> SRS.md, REQUIREMENTS.md  FROM requirements.json   # D-01

for n in 1..3:
    gate = ba-tools verify --reqs requirements.json --reqs-format json --source <source>
    if gate.exit == 2:                       # deterministic FAIL (e.g. CITATION_NOT_FOUND)
        spawn(ba-srs-writer, redraft=gate.failures); continue

    findings = spawn(ba-critic, { source_path, requirements.json path })   # ONLY these (D-21)
    new_fails = [f for f in findings if f.severity == "fail" and f not in prior_fails]
    if not new_fails:
        log("passed early" if n == 1 else f"passed after {n}"); break       # converged (D-11)
    spawn(ba-srs-writer, redraft=findings)                                   # critic is read-only
else:
    escalate_to_confirm(findings)            # 3 loops, still failing -> human (D-10)

ba-tools trace write --kind srs --slug <slug> ...   # TOOL-07
ba-tools index update                                # TOOL-08 (gap/orphan/stale)
```

**Tool Use:**

The agents have **no API tools** in the SDK sense. Their only capabilities are: (a) **read files** (source doc, section splits, `requirements.json`), and (b) the **deterministic `ba-tools` CLI** invoked by the *workflow* (not by the agent autonomously — v1 Codex has no autonomous cross-skill spawn, DESIGN §1). Configuration of these "tools":

- `ba-tools verify --reqs <requirements.json> --reqs-format json --source <doc> [--cite-scope section|document]` — the Quality gate's deterministic half. Reuses `citation_exists` (≥12-char verbatim, section-scoped) and the lint fold verbatim from Phase 1; only the input parser changes from Markdown-table to JSON (D-02).
- `ba-tools trace write` / `index update` — pure file/hash work, registered as new subcommands following the Phase 1 `register(subparsers)` pattern in `__main__.py`.
- **No render tools** this phase — `ba-srs-analyze` is text-only; `mmdc`/draw.io belong to Phases 3+. No new runtime dependency (D-18).
- Path safety: every path input resolves under `--repo-root` via `repo.py` (`resolve_under_root`, `is_within_root`) — row-supplied sources are attacker-influenced data and surface `PATH_TRAVERSAL` as its own auditable FAIL code.

**State Management:**

All durable state lives in `.ba-ops/` (survives `/clear`, git-committable; DESIGN §3/§8):

- `srs/<slug>/requirements.json` — **canonical** source of truth (D-01). `SRS.md` and the `REQUIREMENTS.md` registry are *rendered views*, never hand-maintained — no table↔JSON drift.
- `srs/<slug>/analysis.md` — writer working notes. **The critic must never read this** (D-21) — independence guarantee.
- `traces/<kind>-<slug>.json` — uniform per-artifact trace record (D-05): `kind`, `slug`, `artifact_path`, `source_doc`, `source_hash` (sha256), `req_ids: [{id, statement_hash}]`. `index update` reads *only* these records — never re-parses raw artifacts (D-04).
- `INDEX.md` — the traceability matrix; `index update` rebuilds it and flags **gap** (no downstream coverage), **orphan** (cited REQ-ID with no defining `requirements.json`), **stale** (live `source_hash` ≠ recorded — re-run needed, D-06).
- `STATE.md` — loop counter, last route, gate verdicts, blockers. Writes are **lockfile-guarded** via `filelock` (`FileLock(timeout=10)`, the Phase 1 `state_store.py` pattern) so concurrent operators can't clobber state. `.ba-ops/traces/` writes respect the same guard where concurrent.

**Context Window Strategy:**

**Pass file paths + a small manifest, never raw content** (DESIGN §4, D-21). The workflow hands the writer `{source_path, sections_dir, slug, route}` and the critic `{source_path, requirements.json path}` — the agents `Read` only what they need. This serves two goals at once:

- **Context economy** — the orchestrator stays thin and within byte tiers (workflow < 38,000 B; AGENTS.md/eager refs < 32,768 B or Codex silently truncates).
- **CoVe independence** — restricting the critic's payload to `{source, requirements.json}` is *also* the mechanism that keeps verification independent of the writer's reasoning (the critic literally cannot see `analysis.md`). Context strategy and the core correctness guarantee are the same lever here.

For large sources, the deterministic `extract` step pre-splits the doc into sections under `srs/<slug>/`, so the writer can work section-by-section rather than loading the whole document at once, and citation checks scope to the cited section (cheaper and more precise than document-scope).

## 4b. AI Systems Best Practices

> Written by `gsd-ai-researcher`. Cross-cutting patterns every developer building AI systems needs — adapted honestly to this project's hard constraints (zero new runtime deps; determinism boundary; interactive Codex-chat operator, not a high-throughput service).

### Structured Outputs with Pydantic

**The canonical structured output here is `requirements.json`, and it is validated by the deterministic `ba-tools` CLI — NOT by a Python LLM framework's Pydantic-retry loop.** There is no `.with_structured_output()` / `instructor` / `PydanticOutputParser` / `response_format` integration, because there is no Python LLM SDK in this stack (zero new deps, D-18). Pydantic is still valuable in two places that **don't** cross the determinism boundary: (a) as the **schema contract** documenting the structured-output target, and (b) optionally inside `ba-tools` to validate the JSON shape before the citation/lint gate runs (stdlib `json` is sufficient; if a schema lib were ever added it would be a *plugin-scoped* dev concern, never a spine dep).

```python
# Schema contract for requirements.json (matches D-03). Documents the structured-output
# target the writer must hit. Shown as Pydantic for clarity; the SPINE validates shape
# with stdlib json + ba-tools checks (no new runtime dependency).
from enum import Enum
from pydantic import BaseModel, Field, field_validator


class ReqStatus(str, Enum):
    stated = "stated"      # asserted directly by the source -> citation-checked
    derived = "derived"    # inferred by the writer -> NOT citation-checked (verify_cmd skips)


class SourceTrace(BaseModel):
    doc: str                                   # path to source, resolved under --repo-root
    span: str = Field(min_length=12)           # VERBATIM >=12-char substring of the cited section
    section: str | None = None                 # None -> document-scope search; else section-scoped


class Requirement(BaseModel):
    id: str = Field(pattern=r"^(FR|NFR|BR)-\d{3,}$")   # semantic prefix + sequence (D-09)
    statement: str
    status: ReqStatus
    source_trace: SourceTrace | None = None    # required iff status == stated

    @field_validator("source_trace")
    @classmethod
    def stated_requires_trace(cls, v, info):
        if info.data.get("status") == ReqStatus.stated and v is None:
            raise ValueError("stated requirement must carry a source_trace")
        return v


class RequirementsDoc(BaseModel):
    slug: str
    requirements: list[Requirement]
```

**"Retry-on-invalid" here = the CoVe loop + the deterministic gate, not framework-internal retries.** The validation/repair cycle is:

1. `ba-tools verify --reqs-format json` parses the JSON; a structurally invalid file or a `stated` req whose `span` is not a real ≥12-char verbatim substring of the cited section is a FAIL (`CITATION_NOT_FOUND`, exit 2). `verify_cmd` only citation-checks `status == stated` rows — `derived` reqs are intentionally skipped.
2. **Retry budget = the ≤3 critic loops** (D-10). A FAIL → `ba-srs-writer` re-drafts the offending requirements; the critic adds semantic findings (atomicity, classification, ungrounded-but-passing-substring).
3. **Log:** every gate verdict (`failures` list with codes/req_ids) and every loop outcome (`"passed early"` / `"passed after N"`) to `STATE.md`.
4. **Surface:** after loop 3 still failing → escalate to a human Confirm checkpoint. Never silent-pass.

### Async-First Design

Adapt honestly: **this is an interactive, human-in-the-loop Codex-chat operator, not a high-throughput async service.** There is no event loop in this repo to manage and no `asyncio.run()` to misuse — the host runtime drives turns. So the classic async pitfalls (nested `asyncio.run()`, fire-and-forget tasks) **do not apply** to the agent layer, and inventing an async orchestrator would violate the zero-new-deps and thin-orchestrator constraints.

What the async lesson *maps to* here:

- **Sequential, gated, never concurrent within a slug.** v1 Codex runs writer→verify→critic as one sequential loop (no autonomous cross-skill spawn, DESIGN §1). Each step must complete and its gate verdict be recorded before the next — never overlap writer and critic on the same slug.
- **Stream for UX, await for the gate.** In the chat surface, the agent's reasoning *streams* for visibility (DESIGN §10b — the fail→critic→pass loop is the blast-radius control surface). But the structured artifact must be **fully materialized and the deterministic `ba-tools verify` run to completion** before convergence is judged — never act on a partial/streaming `requirements.json`.
- **Concurrency safety is at the file layer, not the call layer:** `STATE.md`/`.ba-ops/traces/` writes are `filelock`-guarded (`FileLock(timeout=10)`) so two operators on the machine can't clobber shared state.

### Prompt Engineering Discipline

The agent prompt bodies (`ba-srs-writer.md`, `ba-critic.md`) are the real engineering surface. Discipline points specific to grounded extraction + CoVe:

- **System vs. user separation.** The durable role/contract (writer = "emit atomic grounded requirements with verbatim spans"; critic = "re-derive from source only, never read rationale") is the *system*-level prompt body. The per-run *user*-level payload is the thin manifest (`{source_path, …}`, D-21). Keep the contract out of the per-run payload so it can't be diluted or overridden by source content.
- **Grounded-extraction rules (writer), stated explicitly:**
  - **Verbatim span** — copy the `source_trace.span` exactly from the cited section (≥12 chars); never paraphrase or summarize. This is what `citation_exists` enforces.
  - **Atomicity** — one testable requirement per entry; split compound "and/or" statements (the lint atomicity check flags these).
  - **No fabrication** — if the source does not assert it, it is `derived` (and carries no `source_trace`), or it is dropped. Never label an inference `stated`.
  - **Correct classification** — `FR-`/`NFR-`/`BR-` prefix is a judgement the writer owns; uniqueness/stability is enforced deterministically by lint (D-09).
- **CoVe independence discipline (critic):** generate one verification question per requirement, **answer it from the source alone**, then compare the answer to the requirement — the factored CoVe variant (answers not biased by the draft) is what produces the measured hallucination reduction. The prompt must forbid reading `analysis.md`/writer rationale.
- **Few-shot:** prefer a small number of **inline** exemplars in the prompt body (one good `stated`+span, one `derived`, one rejected paraphrase) over dynamic retrieval — the schema is fixed and small, so static exemplars are cheaper and more stable. No retrieval infrastructure is warranted.
- **`max_tokens`:** set an explicit bound on every spawn where the host allows it — never leave unbounded. A runaway writer that emits 200 low-quality requirements or a critic that monologues both waste the loop budget and obscure the findings.

### Context Window Management

This is a **structured-extraction + multi-agent-verification** hybrid; the context strategy combines truncation-by-scoping (extraction side) with payload minimization (multi-agent side):

- **Pre-split, then scope (extraction).** The deterministic `extract` step splits the source into sections under `.ba-ops/srs/<slug>/` (D-17). The writer works section-by-section; citation checks default to **section scope** (`--cite-scope section`) — cheaper and more precise than re-scanning the whole document, and it bounds how much source text any single check loads.
- **Paths-not-content (multi-agent).** The orchestrator never forwards raw artifact bodies between agents — it passes paths + a small manifest (DESIGN §4, D-21). This keeps the workflow file itself within byte tiers (< 38,000 B) and, critically, keeps the critic's window free of the writer's rationale (independence).
- **No conversation summarization / no autonomous compaction.** Unlike a long-running chat or an autonomous agent, the loop is bounded at ≤3 iterations with file-state carrying memory across turns. State persists in `.ba-ops/` (survives `/clear`), so there is no growing transcript to summarize or compact. If a source is large enough to strain a single writer turn, split the *work* by section (above), not the *context* by summary — summaries would break the verbatim-span guarantee.

### Cost and Latency Budget

Adapt honestly: the cost concern is **loop count and re-derivation token use, not per-call price optimization at volume.** This is one BA in a Codex chat producing a handful of SRS artifacts a day — there is no high-throughput billing surface to optimize, and the host runtime owns model pricing (model-agnostic authoring layer). The real budget is:

- **Bounded loops (the primary lever).** Worst case per slug ≈ 1 writer draft + (≤3 × (verify + critic + re-draft)). Each loop re-derives every requirement from the source, so token use scales with `source_size × loops`. The ≤3 cap (D-10) is the hard budget; early-exit-on-convergence (D-11) is the optimization — a clean draft costs **1** loop (`"passed early"`), not 3.
- **Deterministic work is free of model cost.** `ba-tools verify`/`trace write`/`index update` are pure Python (citation substring check, sha256, JSON) — no tokens. Pushing every check that *can* be deterministic into `ba-tools` (rather than asking the model to "double-check") is the cheapest and most reliable path, and it's mandated by the determinism boundary anyway.
- **Caching is structural, not semantic.** `source_hash` (D-06) is an exact-match cache key: if the live source hash matches the recorded one, the artifact is not stale and `ba-srs-analyze` need not re-run for that slug. There is no need for a semantic/embedding cache — the deterministic hash *is* the cache invalidation signal. `statement_hash` (D-12) similarly avoids re-checking unchanged requirements downstream.
- **Cheaper models for sub-tasks:** if the host ever exposes model selection (v2 Claude path), the critic's verification-question answering is a narrower, more constrained task than open-ended drafting and could run a smaller/cheaper model — but in v1 both roles run the single host model, so this is a forward note, not a current optimization.

## 5. Evaluation Strategy

> Built on Section 1b's domain rubric ingredients. The organizing principle is the **determinism split**: every characteristic that `ba-tools verify` can prove by code (verbatim citation, REQ-ID uniqueness/stability, schema shape, statement/source hashing) is a **Code-measured, exit-2 hard gate** at near-zero marginal eval cost — run it on every artifact, no sampling. Everything that requires judgement (atomicity, ambiguity, verifiability, classification, completeness) is split between the **deterministic lint heuristics** (cheap regex/structural proxies, advisory or gating) and an **LLM judge / human** for the residual that heuristics cannot decide. The CoVe-specific dimensions (critic independence, convergence honesty, grounding recall/precision) are the system's signature risk and are evaluated on the reference dataset, not by code.

### Dimensions

Code catches literal "TBD" tokens only. | Medium |

FAIL: critic consults the writer's reasoning → CoVe collapses to self-agreement. | **Code (payload assertion)** the workflow can log/assert the critic's input set excludes `analysis.md`. **LLM judge** on a planted fixture: does the critic's reasoning reference writer-only rationale? **Human** spot-check. | Critical |

That residual is exactly what D4–D8 (lint proxy + judge) and D9–D11 (CoVe) cover. This split is the determinism boundary (DESIGN §5/§11), not an eval convenience.

### Eval Tooling

**Primary tool: pytest fixtures + `ba-tools verify` exit codes (Code dimensions D1–D3, D10) — no new dependency.** The deterministic gate IS the eval harness for the Critical Code dimensions: a fixture corpus under `tests/fixtures/srs/` with expected exit codes and expected `failures[]` codes, asserted by pytest (already the project's test runner, `pyproject.toml`). This honors the zero-new-runtime-deps rule (D-18) and the determinism boundary — the eval of the deterministic layer is itself deterministic and free.

**LLM-judge dimensions (D4–D9, D11): a thin, host-runtime judge prompt — no Python LLM SDK.** Consistent with Section 2 (the host runtime is the executor; there is no orchestration process for RAGAS/Promptfoo to live in, and adding one would violate D-18). The judge is a runtime-agnostic prompt body (sibling to `ba-critic.md`) scored against the labeled reference dataset during the Verify phase, not wired into the CLI. RAGAS/Promptfoo/Phoenix-evals are **explicitly not adopted** — they assume a Python LLM pipeline this architecture does not have. [Inference] this trade-off (no off-the-shelf eval platform) is based on the stated zero-deps + determinism constraints, not a claim that those tools are technically incapable.

## 6. Guardrails

> **Honest framing:** this is an **interactive, human-in-the-loop Codex-chat operator, not a hosted production service.** There is no request stream, no per-user latency SLA, no traffic to rate-limit. So "online/real-time" here means **"runs inside every `ba-srs-analyze --route full` invocation, synchronously, before the artifact is accepted"** — the BA is in the loop and the artifact is not consumed downstream until the gate passes. "Offline/flywheel" means the **accumulating-fixtures + `ba-tools discovery` improvement loop** that folds recurring failures back into the eval set and the agent prompts — not a sampled production-traffic batch job.

### Online (per-invocation, synchronous — runs every `--route full` / `verify`)

| # | Guardrail | Trigger | Intervention |
|---|-----------|---------|--------------|
| G1 | **Deterministic verify gate (the PRIMARY guardrail)** | Any `status:stated` req with a non-verbatim/wrong-section span (`CITATION_NOT_FOUND`), malformed schema, duplicate/unstable REQ-ID, or `PATH_TRAVERSAL` on a source path | **Block — exit 2.** Artifact is rejected; the failures fold back into the writer re-draft. Catches Failure modes #1 (ungrounded), #4 (schema/ID), and path-safety. Fast, free, hash-provable. |
| G2 | **≤3-loop + escalate-to-Confirm (the convergence guardrail)** | A loop produces new FAIL-severity critic findings; loop counter reaches 3 still failing | **Escalate** to a human Confirm checkpoint — never silent auto-pass. Caps re-derivation cost and forces a human decision on non-convergence. Catches Failure mode #3. |
| G3 | **Critic-independence payload contract** | `ba-critic` spawn payload | **Block by construction** — the workflow passes `{source_path, requirements.json path}` ONLY (D-21); `analysis.md` is never in the payload. Assert/log the input set. Catches Failure mode #2. |
| G4 | **Determinism-boundary assertion** | `ba-tools` command code paths (`verify`, `trace write`, `index update`) | **Block at review/CI** — these commands must contain zero LLM calls (DESIGN §5/§11). A unit-test/lint assertion that no model client is imported into `ba_tools/` keeps the gate hash-provable. Catches Failure mode #5. |

Guardrails are kept minimal and ordered cheapest-first (G1 deterministic before G2 model loops) — each loop costs `source_size × tokens`, so the deterministic block runs first and short-circuits.

### Offline (Flywheel — accumulating fixtures + discovery, not sampled traffic)

| Metric | Sampling / source | Action on degradation |
|--------|-------------------|----------------------|
| **Recurring failure shapes** (e.g. repeated weak-word leakage, recurring FR/NFR mis-classification) | `ba-tools discovery` entries + critic findings logged to `STATE.md`, reviewed by the operator-maintainer (Section 1b) | Promote the recurring shape into a **new fixture** in `tests/fixtures/srs/` and tighten the lint heuristic or sharpen the writer/critic prompt. The `iterate` route (D-16) already folds accumulated discovery entries + prior critic findings into a re-draft. |
| **Critic recall/precision drift (D11)** | Periodic re-score of the labeled fixture set with the current judge prompt | If recall on planted-bad fixtures (F2–F8) drops or precision on F1 drops, recalibrate the critic/judge prompt and re-run the human adjudication. |
| **Judge-vs-human correlation** | Verify-phase calibration sample of LLM-judge dimensions (D4–D8, D11) | If correlation < 0.7, the judge is not yet trustworthy — fall back to human review for that dimension and revise the rubric/prompt until calibrated. |
| **Convergence-loop distribution** | `"passed early"` vs `"passed after N"` vs escalation counts logged in `STATE.md` | A rising share of `"passed after 2/3"` or escalations signals the writer prompt is drafting weaker first passes — feed the common findings back into the writer's grounded-extraction rules (§4b). |

## 7. Production Monitoring

**Tracing Tool — Arize Phoenix evaluated and DECLINED as a mandatory dependency; OVERRIDE chosen.**

The GSD default is Arize Phoenix (self-hostable, OpenTelemetry). It does not fit here, for reasons specific to this architecture — stated explicitly as the trade-off:

- **No hosted service, no long-running process.** This is a local CLI operator driven by the host chat. There is no Python orchestration process or web service for an OTel/Phoenix collector to instrument; the "production" surface is one BA running `ba-srs-analyze` a handful of times a day, locally, committing artifacts to git.
- **Zero-new-runtime-deps (D-18) + determinism boundary (DESIGN §5/§11).** `pip install arize-phoenix opentelemetry-sdk` adds runtime dependencies the spine forbids, and wiring a tracer into `ba_tools/` would risk leaking non-deterministic/IO behavior into the hash-provable gate. Phoenix instruments LLM SDK calls — but there is no LLM SDK in this stack (Section 2).

**Chosen trace substrate (the lightest honest alternative): `ba-tools`-emitted structured records in `.ba-ops/` + `STATE.md`.** The system already persists durable, git-committable, hash-provable state that *is* the trace:

- `STATE.md` (lockfile-guarded) — loop counter, last route, every gate verdict, `"passed early"` / `"passed after N"` / escalation, blockers. This is the convergence trace.
- `.ba-ops/traces/<kind>-<slug>.json` (D-05) — per-artifact `source_hash` + `req_ids[{id, statement_hash}]`. This is the provenance/drift trace.
- `INDEX.md` (D-13) — the gap/orphan/stale roll-up; the standing health view of the traceability spine.
- `ba-tools verify` JSON envelope (`{ok, failures[]}`) — every gate decision, with codes and req_ids, printed to stdout and recordable.

**Phoenix remains an OPTIONAL, local-only dev aid** — a developer iterating on the writer/critic prompts in a v2 Claude-SDK path may run `px.launch_app()` (http://localhost:6006) to visualize traces locally. It must never become a spine runtime dependency or be wired into `ba_tools/`. [Inference] this recommendation is based on the project's stated constraints, not a claim that Phoenix would fail technically.

**Key Metrics to Track** (read from `STATE.md` / traces / `INDEX.md` — no external collector):
1. **Gate FAIL rate by code** — `CITATION_NOT_FOUND` vs schema vs stability vs `PATH_TRAVERSAL` frequency across runs (Failure modes #1, #4, #5).
2. **Convergence-loop distribution** — share of `"passed early"` vs `"passed after N"` vs escalations (Failure mode #3 + writer first-pass quality).
3. **Critic recall/precision on the fixture set** — re-scored on a cadence (D11; Failure mode #1).
4. **INDEX drift counts** — open gaps / orphans / stale entries over time (traceability-spine health).
5. **Critic-independence assertion pass rate** — fraction of runs where the payload-contract check confirmed `analysis.md` was excluded (Failure mode #2).

**Alert Thresholds** (advisory for a local internal tool — these prompt human review, they do not page an on-call):
- **Any escalation-to-Confirm that was overridden to pass without addressing the FAIL** → review immediately (this is the silent-auto-pass risk materializing).
- **Critic recall on planted-bad fixtures (F2–F8) < 1.0** → the critic is missing seeded failures; recalibrate before trusting it.
- **Judge-vs-human correlation < 0.7** on any LLM-judge dimension → stop trusting that judge; fall back to human review.
- **Rising orphan/stale counts in INDEX.md** → upstream drift not being re-run; investigate the `iterate` cadence.

**Smart Sampling Strategy** (signal-weighted, per Section 1b's operator-maintainer role): the spine is low-volume, so sample for human review by **concerning signal**, not at random — prioritize runs that (a) escalated to Confirm, (b) `"passed after 3"` (barely converged), (c) emitted any WARN-severity weak-word/ambiguity finding, or (d) flipped a previously-clean slug to `stale`. Watch for **signal–metric divergence**: if a BA repeatedly re-runs `iterate` on a slug the gate calls "passed," the gate is missing something the human sees — that divergence is the early-warning signal to add a fixture and tighten a rubric (feeds the Section 6 flywheel).
