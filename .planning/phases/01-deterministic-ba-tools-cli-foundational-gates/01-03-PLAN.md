<objective>
Implement the lockfile gate (TOOL-03): `ba-tools state update|patch|advance` writes
`.ba-ops/STATE.md` guarded by `filelock.FileLock(str(lock_path), timeout=10)` (D-01/D-02
— `filelock`, NOT raw `O_EXCL`), with Windows-safe stale-lock reclaim (mtime > 10s ->
`os.remove`, where the `PermissionError` is the live-lock sentinel, per RESEARCH Pattern 2
+ Pitfall 1). Two concurrent writers must never clobber each other: the loser either waits
and then succeeds, or raises `filelock.Timeout` -> `BaToolsError` (exit 2, LOCK_TIMEOUT).

Purpose: this is the third foundational gate and the spine's concurrency-safety guarantee
(DESIGN §8). Every later operator's state writes ride on this. It is the heaviest single
verification in the phase (the concurrent-write test dominates the ~15s suite runtime).
Output: `state_store.py` (lock + merge) + `state_cmd.py` + the multiprocessing concurrent-write test.
</objective>

| T-1-03 | Tampering | `state_store.acquire_state_lock` stale reclaim | mitigate | `os.remove` on a live lock raises PermissionError (Windows holds open handles) and is swallowed; an attacker cannot keep a lock alive without an open handle, so it falls through to FileLock's timed wait |

| T-1-07 | Information Disclosure | Timeout / merge errors | mitigate | Errors surface as structured `{code: LOCK_TIMEOUT|BAD_DATA}` to stderr + exit 2; no traceback (inherited from 01-01 handler) |
